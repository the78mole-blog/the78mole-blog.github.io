### Adding the secret sauce to release the cable

To release the cable, you need to open the loop with the diode and the three resistors. For that purpose and to not get into trouble with moisture, I decided to use some reed-contact (ensure to get a NC-type = normally-closed). So I ordered one from Reichelt ([MK15 B2 OE](https://www.reichelt.de/de/de/shop/produkt/reed-sensor_180_v_0_5_a_effner-151845)), added some wires and glued it to the inner side of the type-2-plug using [UHU 2K (fast) glue](https://www.uhu.de/de/produkt.62976).

### Finishing the cable

Since the friend of mine need it to recharge his van battery, he already has a CEE blue connector on his van and also some CEE blue extension cable, replacing the Schuko with a CEE blue was the only reasonable choice. When I opened the Schuko socket, I found flexible cables without end sleeves :-( So it was a great decision to open the socket and make it compliant to VDE.
